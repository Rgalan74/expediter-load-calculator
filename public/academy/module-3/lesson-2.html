Estados Buenos Para Entrar vs Estados Buenos Para Salir

📖 11 min lectura 🎓 Módulo 3 - Lección 2

🔄 EL CONCEPTO DE "DIRECCIÓN DEL FLUJO"

No todos los estados son simétricos. Algunos son:

  • ✅ Excelentes para RECIBIR freight (buenos rates entrando)
  • ❌ Terribles para SALIR (malos rates, poco freight saliendo)

Y viceversa.

📊 LA MATRIZ DE FLUJO

Categoría Característica Ejemplos
BALANCED 🟢🟢 Buen freight IN & OUT
Best case scenario		 Illinois (Chicago), Georgia (Atlanta), Texas (Dallas/Houston), Michigan (Detroit), Ohio
IMPORT-HEAVY 🟢🔴 Good freight IN
Poor freight OUT		 California (LA ports), Florida (consumer goods), Arizona (Phoenix distribution)
DEAD ZONES 🔴🔴 Poor freight IN & OUT
Avoid unless premium		 Montana, Wyoming, West Texas (Laredo, El Paso), Rural Midwest

STRATEGY:

  • Balanced: Build circuit around these
  • Import-heavy: Charge premium going IN, have backhaul plan
  • Dead zones: Only go if rate compensates repositioning + wait

🗺️ FREIGHT FLOW PATTERNS

WEST → EAST (Import dominance)

  • LA/Long Beach ports → Midwest/East Coast
  • High rates westbound (empty returns)
  • Lower rates eastbound (competitive)
  • Strategy: Favor eastbound loads

HUBS (Balanced in all directions)

  • Chicago, Atlanta, Dallas
  • Consistent freight 360°
  • "Safe haven" zones
  • Strategy: Always profitable to position here

📍 ANÁLISIS ESTADO POR ESTADO

🟢🟢 TIER 1: BALANCED STATES

ILLINOIS (Chicago)

  • IN: 🟢🟢 Manufacturing, ag, distribution
  • OUT: 🟢🟢 Consumer goods, auto parts
  • Avg rate IN: $1.55/mi
  • Avg rate OUT: $1.50/mi
  • Note: Weekend freight slower

GEORGIA (Atlanta)

  • IN: 🟢🟢 Distribution, imports via Savannah
  • OUT: 🟢🟢 Manufacturing, distribution
  • Avg rate IN: $1.60/mi
  • Avg rate OUT: $1.55/mi
  • Note: Q4 is insane for outbound

MICHIGAN (Detroit)

  • IN: 🟢🟢 Auto parts, manufacturing
  • OUT: 🟢🟢 Automotive, ag
  • Avg rate IN: $1.65/mi
  • Avg rate OUT: $1.60/mi
  • Note: Q1 retooling = golden period

🟢🔴 TIER 2: IMPORT-HEAVY

CALIFORNIA (LA/Oakland)

  • IN: 🟢🟢 High demand, ports busy
  • OUT: 🔴 Expensive, competitive
  • Avg rate IN: $1.70/mi
  • Avg rate OUT: $1.10/mi (ouch)
  • Note: Charge premium IN unless backhaul to AZ/NV

FLORIDA (Miami/Orlando/Tampa)

  • IN: 🟢 Consumer goods, seasonal
  • OUT: 🔴 Limited manufacturing
  • Avg rate IN: $1.50/mi (seasonal $1.80 in winter)
  • Avg rate OUT: $1.05/mi
  • Note: Q1 snowbird season = good IN rates

🔴🔴 TIER 3: DEAD ZONES

WEST TEXAS (Laredo, El Paso)

  • IN: 🔴 Border towns, low freight
  • OUT: 🔴🔴 Terrible backhaul
  • Avg rate OUT: $0.85-1.10/mi
  • Wait time: 48-96 hours
  • Strategy: Charge $1.80+ to go there, plan repositioning

💰 ESTRATEGIA DE PRICING POR DIRECCIÓN

De → A Strategy
Balanced → Balanced Standard rates ($1.40-1.60/mi)
Balanced → Import-Heavy Premium +$0.20/mi (compensate bad backhaul)
Import-Heavy → Balanced Can accept lower ($1.20-1.35) - escaping dead zone
Balanced → Dead Zone Premium +$0.35-0.50/mi (compensate reposition + wait)
Dead Zone → Anywhere Break-even acceptable (just getting out)

💡 CONCLUSIÓN CLAVE

No todos los estados son iguales

Algunos son highways, otros son dead-ends

REGLA DE ORO:

  • Build circuit around BALANCED states
  • Charge PREMIUM to go to import-heavy states
  • Have EXIT PLAN before entering dead zones
  • Favor LANES (not just destinations)

Think in CYCLES, not one-way trips

Great States to Enter vs Great States to Exit

📖 11 min read 🎓 Module 3 - Lesson 2

🔄 THE CONCEPT OF "FREIGHT DIRECTION"

Not all states are created equal. Some places are:

  • ✅ Amazing to drive INTO (High rates on the way there)
  • ❌ Absolutely terrible to drive OUT OF (Trash rates, zero freight leaving)

And exactly the reverse is true for others.

📊 THE FREIGHT FLOW MATRIX

Category Defining Traits Classic Examples
BALANCED 🟢🟢 Strong freight IN & OUT
The ultimate best case scenario.		 Illinois (Chicago), Georgia (Atlanta), Texas (Dallas/Houston), Michigan (Detroit), Ohio
IMPORT-HEAVY 🟢🔴 Good freight paying well to go IN.
Very poor freight trying to leave.		 California (LA ports), Florida (consumer goods), Arizona (Phoenix distribution)
DEAD ZONES 🔴🔴 Poor freight going IN & OUT.
Avoid unless they pay a massive premium.		 Montana, Wyoming, West Texas (Laredo, El Paso), Rural Midwest

YOUR PLAYBOOK STRATEGY:

  • Balanced: Build your primary driving circuits around these states.
  • Import-heavy: Charge a massive premium to go IN, and have your backhaul plan ready before you accept.
  • Dead zones: Only go if the rate fully covers your crazy repositioning and wait time.

🗺️ MASTERING FREIGHT FLOW PATTERNS

WEST → EAST (The Import Dominance)

  • LA/Long Beach ports moving goods → Midwest/East Coast
  • High rates heading westbound (brokers need vans to take empties back)
  • Lower rates heading eastbound (hyper-competitive market)
  • Strategy: Position yourself to ride the eastbound wave when possible.

THE MAJOR HUBS (Balanced in all directions)

  • Chicago, Atlanta, Dallas
  • Consistent, reliable freight 360° around the compass.
  • These are your "Safe Haven" zones.
  • Strategy: It is almost always a profitable move to reposition yourself towards a hub.

📍 STATE-BY-STATE BREAKDOWN

🟢🟢 TIER 1: THE BALANCED STATES

ILLINOIS (Chicago Area)

  • IN: 🟢🟢 Heavy manufacturing, agriculture, distribution centers.
  • OUT: 🟢🟢 Huge consumer goods and auto parts heading out.
  • Average rate IN: $1.55/mi
  • Average rate OUT: $1.50/mi
  • Note: Freight slows down considerably over the weekend.

GEORGIA (Atlanta Area)

  • IN: 🟢🟢 Massive distribution hub, heavy imports coming via Savannah.
  • OUT: 🟢🟢 Thriving manufacturing and distribution.
  • Average rate IN: $1.60/mi
  • Average rate OUT: $1.55/mi
  • Note: Q4 (holidays) is absolutely insane for outbound freight.

MICHIGAN (Detroit Area)

  • IN: 🟢🟢 Auto parts demanding just-in-time delivery.
  • OUT: 🟢🟢 Automotive assembly components and agriculture.
  • Average rate IN: $1.65/mi
  • Average rate OUT: $1.60/mi
  • Note: Q1 factory retooling is the golden period here.

🟢🔴 TIER 2: IMPORT-HEAVY TRAPS

CALIFORNIA (LA/Oakland)

  • IN: 🟢🟢 Extremely high demand, ports are always bustling.
  • OUT: 🔴 Very expensive, hyper-competitive to find a load out.
  • Average rate IN: $1.70/mi
  • Average rate OUT: $1.10/mi (ouch)
  • Note: Always charge a heavy premium to go IN, unless you have a guaranteed backhaul to AZ/NV waiting.

FLORIDA (Miami/Orlando/Tampa)

  • IN: 🟢 Lots of consumer goods, very seasonal.
  • OUT: 🔴 Barely any outbound manufacturing to support trucks.
  • Average rate IN: $1.50/mi (jumps to $1.80+ during winter).
  • Average rate OUT: $1.05/mi
  • Note: Snowbird season (Q1) brings fantastic inbound rates.

🔴🔴 TIER 3: THE DEAD ZONES

WEST TEXAS (Laredo, El Paso)

  • IN: 🔴 Border towns, surprisingly low inbound demand.
  • OUT: 🔴🔴 The backhaul market is a nightmare.
  • Average rate OUT: $0.85 - $1.10/mi
  • Wait time: Easily 48-96 hours sitting idle.
  • Strategy: Demand $1.80+/mi just to go there, and financially plan for your long repositioning drive.

💰 SMART PRICING BASED ON DIRECTION

From → To Your Negotiation Strategy
Balanced → Balanced Aim for standard profitable rates ($1.40 - $1.60/mi).
Balanced → Import-Heavy Demand a Premium +$0.20/mi (to compensate for the brutal backhaul ahead).
Import-Heavy → Balanced You can safely accept a slightly lower rate ($1.20 - $1.35) — you're paying to escape a dead zone.
Balanced → Dead Zone Demand a Massive Premium +$0.35-$0.50/mi (to cover your idle wait and repositioning costs).
Dead Zone → Anywhere Break-even is totally acceptable (just get the hell out of there!).

🎯 BUILDING YOUR LANE STRATEGY

Stop thinking just in zones. Start thinking in balanced RUTAS (Lanes):

The Lane Good Both Ways? Average Rate

Chicago ↔ Atlanta YES ✅ $1.55/mi
Atlanta ↔ Charlotte YES ✅ $1.50/mi
Detroit → LA ONE WAY 🔴 $1.75/mi IN
LA → Detroit ONE WAY ✅ $1.20/mi OUT
Anywhere → Laredo ONE WAY ⚠️ $1.80/mi IN
Laredo → Anywhere ONE WAY 🔴 $0.95/mi OUT

💡 THE KEY TAKEAWAY

Not all states are created equal.

Some are massive highways to wealth; others are complete financial dead-ends.

THE GOLDEN RULE:

  • Build your driving circuits entirely around BALANCED states.
  • Charge a heavy PREMIUM to drive into import-heavy states.
  • ALWAYS have an EXIT PLAN locked in before entering a dead zone.
  • Favor balanced LANES rather than chasing one-off destinations.

Think in full CYCLES, not just one-way trips.
