Break-Even: Tu Número de Supervivencia

📖 8 min lectura 🎓 Módulo 1 - Lección 3

🎯 ¿QUÉ ES BREAK-EVEN?

Break-even RPM = El mínimo absoluto que necesitas ganar por milla para NO PERDER dinero

  • ❌ Por debajo de break-even = PIERDES dinero
  • ⚠️ En break-even = No ganas, no pierdes (trabajas gratis)
  • ✅ Sobre break-even = GANAS dinero real

📐 LA FÓRMULA

Break-Even RPM = Costo Fijo/Milla + Costo Variable/Milla

Esto te vino de la Lección 2:

  • Costo Fijo/Milla → Lo calculaste en PASO 3
  • Costo Variable/Milla → Lo calculaste en PASO 4

📊 EJEMPLO COMPLETO PASO A PASO

PASO 1: Calcula Costos Fijos Mensuales

  • Pago de van: $1,500
  • Seguro: $900 ($10,800/año ÷ 12)
  • Licencias/permisos: $125 ($1,500/año ÷ 12)
  • Celular + apps: $115
  • TOTAL COSTOS FIJOS: $2,740/mes

PASO 2: Estima Tus Millas Mensuales

Promedio realista: 8,000 millas/mes

PASO 3: Calcula Costo Fijo por Milla

$2,740 ÷ 8,000 millas = $0.34/milla

PASO 4: Suma Costos Variables

  • Combustible: $0.39/milla
  • DEF: $0.04/milla
  • Mantenimiento: $0.10/milla
  • Llantas: $0.03/milla
  • Tolls/parking: $0.04/milla
  • TOTAL VARIABLE: $0.60/milla

PASO 5: Break-Even RPM

$0.34 + $0.60 = $0.94/milla

📌 ¿QUÉ SIGNIFICA ESTE NÚMERO?

Cualquier load por DEBAJO de $0.94/milla literalmente te hace PERDER dinero

Ejemplos:

  • Load a $0.85/milla: Pierdes $0.09/milla → En 500 millas = -$45 de pérdida
  • Load a $0.94/milla: Ganancia $0 → Cubriste costos pero trabajaste GRATIS
  • Load a $1.20/milla: Ganancia $0.26/milla → En 500 millas = $130 de profit real

🚨 LA TRAMPA DEL "ALGO ES MEJOR QUE NADA"

Pensamiento común:

"No tengo nada, este load paga $0.80/milla, al menos es algo..."

❌ ERROR

Si tu break-even es $0.94/milla:

  • Load de $0.80/milla
  • Pierdes $0.14/milla
  • En 600 millas = -$84

Literalmente pagaste $84 de tu bolsillo para "trabajar" ese día

MEJOR OPCIÓN: Esperar un load rentable o reposicionarte a mejor zona

💡 PERO ESPERA... BREAK-EVEN NO ES TU TARGET

Break-even es tu MÍNIMO ABSOLUTO de supervivencia. Tu target REAL debe ser:

TARGET RPM = Break-Even × 1.30 a 1.40

Si tu break-even es $0.94:

  • Target mínimo: $0.94 × 1.30 = $1.22/milla
  • Target ideal: $0.94 × 1.40 = $1.32/milla
¿Por qué este margen extra?

El 30-40% sobre break-even te da:

  • ✅ Profit REAL (no solo cubrir costos)
  • ✅ Cushion para meses lentos
  • ✅ Fondos para emergencias
  • ✅ Dinero para crecer el negocio
  • ✅ Pago para TI (no solo para el negocio)

📊 IMPACTO DE MILLAS MENSUALES EN BREAK-EVEN

Costos fijos: $2,740/mes | Costos variables: $0.60/milla

Si haces 6,000 millas/mes:

Costo fijo/milla: $2,740 ÷ 6,000 = $0.46

Break-even: $0.46 + $0.60 = $1.06/milla ⚠️

Si haces 8,000 millas/mes:

Costo fijo/milla: $2,740 ÷ 8,000 = $0.34

Break-even: $0.34 + $0.60 = $0.94/milla ✅

Si haces 10,000 millas/mes:

Costo fijo/milla: $2,740 ÷ 10,000 = $0.27

Break-even: $0.27 + $0.60 = $0.87/milla ✅✅

CONCLUSIÓN:

Más millas rentables = menor break-even por milla. Esto te hace más competitivo en pricing.

PERO: Solo si esas millas extras son SOBRE tu break-even. No tiene sentido hacer 10,000 millas a $0.80/milla.

Break-Even: Your Survival Number

📖 8 min read 🎓 Module 1 - Lesson 3

🎯 WHAT IS BREAK-EVEN?

Break-even RPM = The absolute minimum you need to earn per mile to NOT LOSE money

  • ❌ Below break-even = You LOSE money
  • ⚠️ At break-even = You don't gain or lose (you work for free)
  • ✅ Above break-even = You MAKE real money

📐 THE FORMULA

Break-Even RPM = Fixed Cost/Mile + Variable Cost/Mile

These came from Lesson 2:

  • Fixed Cost/Mile → You calculated this in STEP 3
  • Variable Cost/Mile → You calculated this in STEP 4

📊 COMPLETE STEP-BY-STEP EXAMPLE

STEP 1: Calculate Monthly Fixed Costs

  • Van payment: $1,500
  • Insurance: $900 ($10,800/year ÷ 12)
  • Licenses/permits: $125 ($1,500/year ÷ 12)
  • Cell phone + apps: $115
  • TOTAL FIXED COSTS: $2,740/month

STEP 2: Estimate Your Monthly Miles

Realistic average: 8,000 miles/month

STEP 3: Calculate Fixed Cost per Mile

$2,740 ÷ 8,000 miles = $0.34/mile

STEP 4: Add Variable Costs

  • Fuel: $0.39/mile
  • DEF: $0.04/mile
  • Maintenance: $0.10/mile
  • Tires: $0.03/mile
  • Tolls/parking: $0.04/mile
  • TOTAL VARIABLE: $0.60/mile

STEP 5: Break-Even RPM

$0.34 + $0.60 = $0.94/mile

📌 WHAT DOES THIS NUMBER MEAN?

Any load BELOW $0.94/mile literally makes you LOSE money

Examples:

  • Load at $0.85/mile: You lose $0.09/mile → Over 500 miles = -$45 loss
  • Load at $0.94/mile: $0 profit → You covered costs but worked FOR FREE
  • Load at $1.20/mile: $0.26/mile profit → Over 500 miles = $130 real profit

🚨 THE "SOMETHING IS BETTER THAN NOTHING" TRAP

Common thinking:

"I have nothing else, this load pays $0.80/mile, at least it's something..."

❌ WRONG

If your break-even is $0.94/mile:

  • Load at $0.80/mile
  • You lose $0.14/mile
  • Over 600 miles = -$84

You literally paid $84 out of pocket to "work" that day

BETTER OPTION: Wait for a profitable load or reposition to a better zone

💡 BUT WAIT... BREAK-EVEN IS NOT YOUR TARGET

Break-even is your ABSOLUTE SURVIVAL MINIMUM. Your REAL target should be:

TARGET RPM = Break-Even × 1.30 to 1.40

If your break-even is $0.94:

  • Minimum target: $0.94 × 1.30 = $1.22/mile
  • Ideal target: $0.94 × 1.40 = $1.32/mile
Why this extra margin?

The 30-40% above break-even gives you:

  • ✅ REAL profit (not just covering costs)
  • ✅ Cushion for slow months
  • ✅ Emergency funds
  • ✅ Money to grow the business
  • ✅ Pay for YOU (not just the business)

📊 IMPACT OF MONTHLY MILES ON BREAK-EVEN

Fixed costs: $2,740/month | Variable costs: $0.60/mile

If you drive 6,000 miles/month:

Fixed cost/mile: $2,740 ÷ 6,000 = $0.46

Break-even: $0.46 + $0.60 = $1.06/mile ⚠️

If you drive 8,000 miles/month:

Fixed cost/mile: $2,740 ÷ 8,000 = $0.34

Break-even: $0.34 + $0.60 = $0.94/mile ✅

If you drive 10,000 miles/month:

Fixed cost/mile: $2,740 ÷ 10,000 = $0.27

Break-even: $0.27 + $0.60 = $0.87/mile ✅✅

CONCLUSION:

More profitable miles = lower break-even per mile. This makes you more competitive on pricing.

BUT: Only if those extra miles are ABOVE your break-even. There is no point driving 10,000 miles at $0.80/mile.
